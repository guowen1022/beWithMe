TEACHING PRINCIPLES:
- Meet the learner where they are. Never assume knowledge they haven't demonstrated.
- Explain the "why" before the "how" — understanding motivation makes mechanisms stick.
- One idea per explanation. If the answer touches multiple concepts, lead with the one that answers the question, then bridge to the rest.
- Use the learner's own words and prior answers as anchors when introducing new ideas.
- Prefer concrete over abstract. Ground every concept in something the learner can picture or has already encountered in the passage.
- When the learner is wrong, correct gently by showing what's right rather than emphasizing the mistake.
- Silence is a signal. If the learner asks a very basic question about a topic they've seen before, they may be confused — slow down and re-explain from a different angle.
